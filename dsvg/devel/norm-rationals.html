In quantifying the significant digits in numerator and denominator, 
    conver to a normed  form. 
    But what is this exactly.
    Find significant digits in numerator and denominator, plus an exponent,
    base 10.

    100 = 1 E2 (1 significant digit, multiply by 100)
   1010 = 101 E1 (3 sigs, mult by 10)
   1234 = 1234 E0
  10001 = 10001 E0
   3500 = 35 E2
   
   Ways to represent this:
   {i:"35",e:2} == 3500
   {i:"1",e:2} == 100
   {i:"2", e:3, s:-1} == -2000

   multiplying two normed integers:
   a = {i:"35",e:2} b = {i:"2",e:3,s:-1}
   r = a * b;
   r = {i:a.i * b.i,e:a.e + b.e,s:((a.s ? a.s : 1) * (b.s ? b.s : 1))}
