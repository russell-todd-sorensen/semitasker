History of World Tartans

Tartan has a long and fascinating history. The word itself probably derives from tiretaine, a French word for a type of woollen fabric, although the Gaelic breacan (specckled, chequered) is more descriptive. Its origins are highly controversial, however. Some historians have identified the earliest known tartan as a small, 3rd-century fragment of cloth, which was found near the Antonine Wall (see Falkirk, page 99), but this was an isolated find. It is far from clear when clansmen began to adopt the custom of wearing tartan.

There is some evidence to suggest that the earliest setts were district rather than clan, tartans. When Martin Martin made a tour of the the Hervrides in the 1690s he noted the fine plaids of divers colours but linked them with places rather than with people.
